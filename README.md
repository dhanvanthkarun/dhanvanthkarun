### 🌟 Featured Quantum Research

* **🌍 Q-NeuroEpidemic: Quantum Machine Learning for Global Neuro-Epidemiology**
  * **Overview:** An open-source, hybrid classical-quantum neural network architecture designed to predict regional stroke risks and neurological crises.
  * **Architecture:** Powered by **PennyLane** and Gradio, utilizing a Quantum Feature Map and a Variational Quantum Circuit (VQC) to discover complex, non-linear cross-correlations between environmental pollution vectors and neural degradation.
  * **Impact:** Directly targets GeoHealth challenges by processing global public health, environmental (PM2.5), and socioeconomic metrics to scale from individual parameter tuning to batch institutional dataset ingestion.

### 🔬 Core Computational Projects

* **🧠 Neuro-Receptor Excitability Predictor (NREP)**
  * **Overview:** An autonomous computational biology pipeline engineered to bridge the fundamental gap between structural bioinformatics and systemic computational neuroscience.
  * **Architecture:** Automatically queries the RCSB Protein Data Bank, parses raw biophysical geometry via `BioPython`, and routes data through an AI engine (Anthropic Claude 3.5 Haiku) to predict downstream neuro-pharmacological impacts on neural network firing rates.

* **🧬 PANDORA 1.0** *(My First AI Project)*[cite: 1]
  * **Overview:** The foundational AI platform that initiated my advanced computational research track[cite: 1].
  * **Architecture:** Engineered a RAG-driven dry-lab screening platform utilizing Llama 3.2 to accelerate drug discovery targeting Alzheimer's Disease pathways, automating macromolecular docking analysis[cite: 1].

* **🩸 Non-Invasive Glucometer**[cite: 1]
  * **Overview:** Led the technical development of a prototype engineered in C on an Arduino Uno platform to eliminate traditional finger-prick blood sampling[cite: 1].
  * **Architecture:** Spearheaded the integration of safe infrared (IR) optical sensors to evaluate blood glucose concentration through real-time transmissive blood density analysis[cite: 1].
